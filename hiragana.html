Welcome to one of the two Japanese writing systems. Here, we tackle Katakana. Katakana is used for a myriad of things. The most common use of it is for foreign words/non-japanese words--most especially English words. This table is read right to left with the vowels located on the right. As you go through the table you will notice above the consonant. When read along with the vowels its consonant first then the corresponding vowel. On the first column on the right are the vowels (a,i,u,e,o) followed by the row with consonants (ka, ki, ku, ke, ko) respetively. So, without further a do, let’s get started shall we?


What is the use of Hiragana?

Hiragana is used for Japanese function words. It was first developed in the 5th century and up to this day it is still being used. This writing system is phonetic in nature meaning to say that each character represents one character syllable.

Please do take note of the stroke order as well for each japanese character. Each character has number guides to help you figure out which stroke goes first.
